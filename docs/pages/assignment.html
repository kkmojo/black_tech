CSC108H / A1: Tweet Analyzer

This assignment is based on the social network site Twitter. Twitter allows users to read and post tweets that are between 1 and 140 characters long (inclusive). In this assignment, you will be writing functions that we imagine are part of the programs that manage Twitter feeds.

Here are some example tweets:

  • #UofT doctor finds that pets provide insights that can improve patient care http://ow.ly/WHJHL via @CBCNews
  • #UofT startups build a more inclusive and accessible Toronto http://ow.ly/VzE91
  • Good morning, #UofT! DYK Robarts library is the length of a Canadian football field? #TriviaTuesday

Some terminology

  • tweet: A message posted on Twitter that is between 1 and 140 characters long (inclusive).
  • hash symbol: The character that begins a hashtag. On Twitter, the # character is the hash symbol.
  • hashtag: A word or phrase in a tweet that begins with the hash symbol. For example, #UofT and #csc108 are two examples of hashtags on Twitter. Hashtags are used to label important words or terms in a tweet. For our purposes, a hashtag begins with the hash symbol, and contains all characters up to (but not including) a space character, or the end of a tweet. A hashtag is preceded by either a space, or the beginning of a tweet.
  • URL: An address to a resource (like a webpage) on the Internet. For example, http://www.twitter.com and http://ow.ly/VR6CT are URLs. For our purposes, a URL is preceded either by a space, or the beginning of a tweet, and contains all characters up to (but not including) a space character, or the end of a tweet.

For a complete list of Twitter terms, check out the Twitter glossary.
